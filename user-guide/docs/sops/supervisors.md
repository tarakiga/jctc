# Standard Operating Procedures: Supervisors

**Role**: `SUPERVISOR`  
**Purpose**: To oversee investigative teams, ensure quality control, and manage resource allocation.

## Daily Checklist
1.  **Dashboard Review**: Check "Critical Cases" and "Overdue Tasks".
2.  **Workload Balance**: Review the **Team Activity Calendar** to identify overloaded or underutilized investigators.
3.  **Approval Queue**: Check for pending case closure requests or sensitive warrants.

## Core Workflows

### 1. Case Assignment
1.  Receive notification of new case from Intake.
2.  Review case complexity and priority.
3.  Go to **Case Detail > Team**.
4.  **Add Member**: Select the Lead Investigator based on expertise and availability.
5.  **Instruction**: Add a specific note or deadline for the initial report.

### 2. Case Review & Closure
Before a case can be closed:
1.  Verify all **Evidence** has a final disposition (returned, destroyed, or archived).
2.  Check **Tasks** completion status.
3.  Review the **Action Log** for any gaps in activity.
4.  **Approval**: Change status to *Closed* only if all criteria are met.

### 3. Team Management
*   **Leave Requests**: Monitor team availability in the calendar.
*   **Performance**: Use the **Admin > Audit Logs** (if accessible) or case stats to review investigator performance metrics (time to close, evidence accuracy).

## Critical Responsibilities
!!! warning "Oversight"
    *   You are responsible for the **integrity** of your team's investigations.
    *   Regularly spot-check **Chain of Custody** logs for gaps.
    *   Ensure no case remains in *Draft* or *Inactive* status for more than 48 hours without action.
